# Fraud Alerts and Suspicious Activity

Use this procedure when the system flagged suspicious activity, or a customer reports charges they didn't make.

## How alerts work
- Risk monitoring flags unusual transactions and may auto-decline them, sending the customer an alert to confirm.
- A confirmed unauthorized charge is fraud; a charge the customer simply forgot is not.

## Steps
1. Verify the customer's identity carefully — fraud calls are a common social-engineering target.
2. Review the flagged transactions with them and mark each as **recognized** or **fraud**.
3. If any are fraud, block the card, open a fraud claim, and issue a replacement card number.

## For confirmed fraud
- File the fraud claim with the transaction list; provisional credit may apply while it's investigated.
- Advise the customer to reset online-banking credentials if the account may be compromised.

## Escalate when
- The account shows signs of full takeover (changed contact info, new payees).
- Fraud spans multiple linked accounts or exceeds standard claim limits.
