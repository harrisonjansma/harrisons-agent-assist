# Chargeback Disputes

Use this when a customer mentions they've disputed a charge with their bank, or when a chargeback notification appears on an account.

## What a chargeback is
A chargeback is when the customer asks their bank to reverse a charge, rather than requesting a refund from us directly. Once a chargeback is filed, the funds are held and the resolution runs on the bank's timeline (often 30–90 days).

## If the customer is still on the call
- If they *haven't* filed yet and just want their money back, a direct refund is faster for everyone — offer that first (see Refunds and Double Charges).
- If they've already filed, explain that we can't issue a separate refund on top, or they could be credited twice and the bank may claw it back. We respond through the bank's dispute process.

## Handling the dispute internally
- Do not issue a manual refund on a transaction with an open chargeback. Flag the transaction in **Billing → Disputes**.
- Gather evidence for the response: order records, delivery/tracking confirmation, usage logs, and any prior support history showing the customer received what they paid for.
- Submit evidence before the bank's deadline shown on the dispute.

## Account status
- Accounts with an open chargeback may have service paused pending resolution, per policy. Explain this neutrally.
- Repeated chargebacks can lead to account restrictions.

## What to tell the customer
- Be calm and non-accusatory; many disputes are honest confusion.
- Set expectations on the bank-driven timeline.

## Escalate
Always route open chargebacks to the Billing/Disputes team — front-line agents do not resolve these directly.
