# Late Payments and Fees

Use this procedure when a customer has a late fee, missed a due date, or wants a fee waived.

## How late fees work
- A late fee posts when the minimum payment isn't received by the due date.
- A missed payment can also affect the account's standing and, past 30 days, may be reported to credit bureaus.

## Steps
1. Open the account and confirm the missed due date, the minimum owed, and whether a fee posted.
2. Take the payment or set one up (see *Making a Payment*) to bring the account current.
3. Check waiver eligibility — a first-time or otherwise on-time customer often qualifies for a one-time courtesy waiver.

## Fee waivers
- Apply the waiver in the console; it reflects on the next statement.
- Set expectations that courtesy waivers are limited and not guaranteed repeatedly.

## Escalate when
- The account is more than 60 days past due or already reported to a bureau.
- The customer requests removal of an accurate late mark from their credit report.
