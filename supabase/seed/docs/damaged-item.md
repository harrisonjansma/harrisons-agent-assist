# Damaged or Defective Items

Use this when a customer receives an item that arrived broken, defective, or not working as expected.

## Gather details
1. Confirm the order number and which item is affected.
2. Ask what's wrong specifically — physical damage, missing parts, or a functional defect.
3. Request one or two photos when there's visible damage. Attach them to the case for warranty and carrier claims.

## Resolve
- **Arrived damaged (shipping):** offer an immediate replacement of the same item. File a carrier damage claim in the background; the customer should not have to wait on that claim to get a working item.
- **Defective on arrival (DOA):** offer a replacement or full refund at the customer's choice. No need to troubleshoot extensively for a clearly DOA unit.
- **Missing parts/accessories:** ship the missing components rather than the whole item when possible.

## Returns
- For most items under $75, issue the replacement without requiring the damaged unit back (return-less resolution).
- For higher-value items, email a prepaid return label; the replacement can still ship right away.

## What to tell the customer
- Apologize for the experience and confirm the resolution and timeline.
- Provide the replacement order number or refund confirmation.

## Warranty note
If the item worked initially and failed later, this is a warranty matter rather than damage-on-arrival — see the Warranty Claims procedure.

## Escalate when
- The defect caused injury or property damage — escalate immediately as a safety issue (see Escalation Policy).
- The customer reports repeated defective units on the same order.
