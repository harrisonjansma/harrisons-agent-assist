# Interest Charges and APR Explained

Use this procedure when a customer questions an interest charge or wants to understand how their APR is applied.

## How interest works
- APR is the yearly rate; interest is calculated daily on the average balance and posted each cycle.
- Paying the **full statement balance** by the due date each cycle generally avoids interest on purchases (the grace period).

## Steps
1. Open the statement and show the interest line and the balance it was charged on.
2. Explain that carrying any balance past the due date removes the grace period until it's paid in full again.
3. Point out cash advances and balance transfers may accrue interest immediately with no grace period, often at a different APR.

## Reducing interest
- Encourage paying more than the minimum; the minimum barely covers accruing interest.
- Note that a promotional APR, if any, reverts to the standard rate on its end date.

## Escalate when
- The interest math doesn't match the disclosed APR and balances.
- The customer requests an APR reduction or hardship rate change.
