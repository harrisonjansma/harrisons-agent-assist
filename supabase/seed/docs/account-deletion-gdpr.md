# Account Deletion and Data Requests

Use this for customers who want to delete their account or exercise a data privacy right (access, export, or erasure under GDPR/CCPA).

## Verify identity first
Data requests require identity verification. Confirm the account email and have the customer complete a verification step (emailed confirmation link). Never action a deletion or data export on an unverified request.

## Types of request
- **Access / export:** the customer wants a copy of their data. Trigger an export from **Admin → Privacy → Export**; the customer receives a download link, usually within 24–48 hours.
- **Deletion / erasure:** the customer wants their account and personal data removed.

## Deletion procedure
1. Explain the consequences: deletion is permanent, cancels any active subscription, and removes access to past orders, notes, and history.
2. If they have an active paid subscription, confirm whether they also want a cancellation/refund handled first (see Cancelling a Subscription).
3. Submit the deletion via **Admin → Privacy → Delete account**.
4. There is a 30-day grace/soft-delete period during which the account can be restored on request. After that, personal data is purged from primary systems, with backups aging out per the retention schedule.

## Legal holds and exceptions
- Some records (transaction/tax records) are retained as required by law even after deletion; explain this is limited to what's legally mandated.
- If there's an open dispute or investigation, deletion may be delayed until it resolves.

## Timelines
Privacy requests are fulfilled within the statutory window (generally 30 days). Confirm the request is logged and give the customer a reference number.

## Escalate
Route anything citing a specific regulation or a regulator complaint to the Privacy/Legal team.
