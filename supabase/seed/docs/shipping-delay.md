# Shipping Delays and Lost Packages

Use this when a customer's order hasn't arrived, is stuck in tracking, or appears lost.

## Locate the order
1. Ask for the order number or the email on the order.
2. Open **Orders** and check the fulfillment status and carrier tracking.
3. Note the promised delivery window versus today's date.

## If it's still within the window
- Reassure the customer of the expected delivery date.
- Explain that tracking can stall for 24–48 hours between scans, especially over weekends and holidays, without meaning the package is lost.

## If it's past the window
- For a package showing **in transit** but overdue by 3+ business days, open a carrier trace from **Orders → Shipping → Start trace**. Traces take 1–2 business days.
- For a package marked **delivered** that the customer didn't receive, ask them to check with neighbors/household and around the property, then wait 24 hours (carriers sometimes scan early).
- If still missing after that, treat as lost: offer a replacement shipment or a full refund.

## Replacements
- Replacements ship at no cost via expedited service when the original is confirmed lost.
- If the item is out of stock, offer a refund or a backorder with an ETA.

## What to tell the customer
- Give a concrete next step and timeline, not just "please wait."
- Provide the trace/case number so they can follow up.

## Escalate when
- The order is high value (over $500) or a bulk/business order.
- The carrier confirms damage in transit (see Damaged or Defective Items).
