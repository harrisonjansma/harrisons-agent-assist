# Cancelling a Subscription

Follow this when a customer wants to cancel a recurring subscription or stop auto-renewal.

## Before cancelling
1. Confirm the account email and which plan they're on under **Account → Subscription**.
2. Ask whether they want to cancel immediately or at the end of the current billing period. Cancelling at period end lets them keep access they've already paid for.
3. Briefly ask the reason — cost, missing feature, switching, or just taking a break. Log it; this feeds retention reporting.

## Retention options (offer once, do not pressure)
- If cost is the blocker, mention the option to downgrade to a cheaper plan (see Plan Upgrades and Downgrades).
- A one-time pause of up to 3 months is available for annual subscribers.

## Perform the cancellation
- Use **Account → Subscription → Cancel**. Choose "End of period" unless the customer explicitly asks for immediate cancellation.
- Immediate cancellation revokes access right away; the customer is **not** automatically refunded for the unused portion unless they qualify under the refund policy.
- Auto-renewal is turned off automatically on cancellation.

## Confirm
- Tell the customer the exact date their access ends.
- Confirm they will not be billed again and that they'll receive a cancellation email.
- Let them know their data is retained for 30 days if they change their mind, after which it may be purged (see Account Deletion and Data Requests).

## Escalate when
- The customer is disputing a renewal charge that already posted.
- The account is a team/enterprise plan with multiple seats — those cancel through the account owner only.
