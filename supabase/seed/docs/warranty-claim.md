# Warranty Claims

Use this when an item that worked initially has failed within its warranty period. (For items broken or defective on arrival, use Damaged or Defective Items instead.)

## Confirm coverage
1. Ask for the order number or proof of purchase and the date received.
2. Standard warranty is **12 months** from delivery unless the product lists a different term. Confirm the failure date falls inside the window.
3. Warranty covers manufacturing defects and normal-use failures. It does **not** cover accidental damage, misuse, unauthorized repairs, or normal wear (e.g., consumables).

## Troubleshoot briefly
- Run the basic troubleshooting for the product category (power cycle, cable/charge check, factory reset). Many "failures" resolve here and save the customer a return.
- Document what was tried.

## Process the claim
- If the defect is confirmed and covered, offer a repair, replacement, or store credit per product policy — replacement is standard for most items under $150.
- Email a prepaid return label for the defective unit. For higher-value items, the replacement ships once the return is scanned by the carrier; for lower-value items, use return-less resolution.
- Log the claim under **Orders → Warranty** with the defect description and troubleshooting notes.

## Out-of-warranty
- If outside the window, explain options: paid repair, discount toward a replacement, or trade-in where available.
- Use judgment for failures just past the window; a one-time courtesy may apply — log the reason.

## What to tell the customer
- Confirm what's covered, the resolution chosen, and the timeline.
- Provide the warranty case number.

## Escalate when
- The failure is a safety hazard (overheating, smoke) — escalate immediately (see Escalation Policy).
