# Refunds and Double Charges

Use this procedure when a customer reports being charged twice, charged the wrong amount, or requests a refund for a recent purchase.

## Confirm the charge
1. Ask for the email on the account and the approximate date and amount of the charge.
2. Open **Billing → Transactions** and search by email. Duplicate charges appear as two identical authorizations within a short window, often minutes apart.
3. A *pending* duplicate authorization is not a real charge — it usually drops off within 3–5 business days on its own. Explain this to the customer and offer to note the account.

## Issue the refund
- For a genuine duplicate *settled* charge, issue a full refund of the extra charge via **Billing → Transactions → Refund**. Select "Duplicate charge" as the reason.
- Refunds return to the original payment method. Card refunds take 5–10 business days depending on the bank; ACH takes 3–5.
- For a partial refund (wrong amount), refund only the difference.

## What to tell the customer
- Confirm the refund amount and the method it returns to.
- Give the expected timeline and let them know they'll get an email receipt.
- If the customer was double-charged due to a retry after a failed payment, apologize and confirm only one charge will stand.

## Escalate when
- The disputed amount is over $500.
- The customer has already filed a chargeback with their bank (see the Chargeback Disputes procedure).
- The transaction is older than 120 days and falls outside the standard refund window.
